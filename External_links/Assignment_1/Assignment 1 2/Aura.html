The AURA BACKGROUND


Launched on July 15, 2004, AURA is an Earth Science satellite owned and operated by NASA. Although it was only intended to last six years, it has been in use for more than 15 years. As a component of the global Observing System (EOS) constellation of spacecraft, the Aura mission investigates the dynamics and chemistry of Earth's atmosphere from the mesosphere to the surface.


The OMI (Ozone Monitoring Instrument) is a type of instrument which is installed in Aura (Spacecraft by NASA) which looks straight towards the Ultraviolet and Visible Spectrum making an image spectrograph which measure the solar radiation backscattered by Earth's atmosphere and surface over the entire wavelength range from 270 to 500 nm with a spectral resolution of 0.5 nm and width of 2600 KM.


Learn More on AURA !!


NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland, oversaw the science investigations, launch, and instrumentation of the EOS Aura satellite. The satellite was intended to stay in operation for at least five years when it was launched in July 2004. Among the scientific payloads were:


1. HIRDLS - High Resolution Dynamics Limb Sounder


2. MLS - Microwave Limb Sounder


3. OMI - Ozone Monitoring Instrument


4. TES - Tropospheric Emission Spectrometer



Ozone Monitoring Instrument (OMI):


With a spectral resolution of roughly 0.5 nm and a swath width of 2600 km, the OMI instrument on board Aura is a nadir-viewing Ultraviolet/Visible spectrophotometry imaging spectrograph that measures the solar radiation backscattered by Earth's atmosphere and surface over the entire wavelength range from 270 to 500 nm. The Finnish Meteorological Institute (FMI) and the
Netherlands Agency for Aerospace Programmes (NIVR) collaborated to provide OMI.
