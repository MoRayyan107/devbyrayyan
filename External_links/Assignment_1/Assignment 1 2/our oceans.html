Intro

One of the main areas being impacted by climate change is our Ocean: the sea is heating up; the ice caps are melting; marine life is dying, and islands of rubbish are being formed. However, with the ever-advancing technologies of today solutions are being found which will be able to aid in the reduction of the effects of climate change on our oceans.

Oceans heating up

Our oceans are rapidly heating up at a faster rate than ever before and we need to do something about it before irreparable damage is done to our planet. In particular the North Atlantic Ocean has been heating up at an insane rate currently compared to every other ocean. This was initially quite confusing to experts in the field, as they expect the temperature to increase for normal reasons such as greenhouse gases trapping the heat from the sun in our atmosphere, but this would not lead to this sharp of an increase in temperature in only one specific ocean. This made researchers start to think about what may be causing this and an area they were correct in researching was the trails given off by ships and planes over the ocean.

NASA scientists used artificial intelligence-based image recognition technology on 20 years worth of data from their Ailing Terra satellites. They used this artificial intelligence to look at the clouds formed by the ships and planes and the way they have changed over the years. They discovered that the clouds produced in recent years stay around for much less time and don’t interact with natural clouds in the same way anymore because of the regulatory changes in what is allowed to be in their fuels. They found that the main cause of these changes in the clouds is the reduction in sulfur content of fuel “Regulations imposed in 2020 by the United Nations’s International Maritime Organization (IMO) have cut ships’ sulfur pollution by more than 80% and improved air quality worldwide.”(Voosen, 2023) This reduction in sulfur is great for air quality and the general health of the planet however an unintended side effect of this is that sulphate particles from the old fules are no longer seeding in low lying clouds which were making them more reflective. These clouds being less reflective now is making it so more heat from the sun is able to make it directly to the ocean heating it up at an alarming rate.

Solutions to this are still in the works but preliminary tests are showing that by injecting clouds with salt particles to make them more reflective might help slow this heating of the ocean so we can find better solutions before we are past the tipping point of global warming

Litter in our oceans

Litter in our oceans is a major concern for the climate because it means that more plastic needs to be manufactured leading to even more greenhouse gases being released into our atmosphere. Just telling people to “stop littering,” is not doing enough as islands of plastic as as large as the state of Texas are forming in our ocean (Pyrek, 2016). A solution that many have come up with is using autonomous underwater vehicles (AUVs) to clean the ocean of marine plastics, and while this works well on a small scale, we have been struggling to make it work in a large scale operation.

Researchers from Westlake University, Hangzhou have been coming up with a way in which many AUV’s can work together to clear areas of the ocean, but this is particularly difficult as the conditions for communication are not ideal. These researchers have come up with a reliable multi-robot task alocation (MRTA) model, which will make it so communication between multiple AUVs is not needed for a large scale operation. In this model, there is a controlling center which the AUVs report to, there they get allocated their individual task which will then be carried out efficiently and no AUVs will interfere with eachother. This mean each robot will collect plastic from its own area and take it to be recycled and when done on scale this will be great in reducing the plastic in our ocean.

While in tests and simulations this solution looks like a great way to reduce the quantitiy of plastic in our ocean, currently the technology in the AUVs is not quite advanced enough yet “there are no powerful underwater robots designed for cleaning marine plastics in the market.” (Hong, Cui and Chen, 2021) . So once the technology has been developed, this looks like an area which will make an impact on the oceans plastics.

Monitoring biodiversity

Computing is also playing a major role in the monitoring of marine biodiversity, this is important for our climate as the species diversity and the abundance of a species tells us a lot about the conditions of an environment. The problems with this monitoring until recently is the sheer scale of the ocean and the amount of data that can be collected. AI is very important in this field of monitoring marine life because there is no real feasible manual way to do it. With this knowledge, “researchers have been working to build monitoring solutions for automatic identification, classification and prediction using AI algorithms.”(Jiang and Zhu, 2022) and with these models created it will make it so other researchers will be able to search for data way easier as data will be recorded and presented in a useable format.

This data will make it so scientists can go look in depth at specific species and see why they are either thriving or failing to adapt to their changing climates. This gives a better understanding of the things we should be worried about happening at scale in our oceans making it hard for ocean life to continue as it is.

The oceans of the world take up over 70% of our planet and it seems like many do not care about the effects of climate change on it. We need to continue to look for solutions with the advancement of technology which will help to slow down the effects of climate change in the short term and figure out the ways to stop the world from hitting a point of no return. While the solutions brought up in this section will not completely change the direction that our world is heading in, they will will play a role in bettering our planets ecosystems, giving more time for more research to be done and other solutions to be found.
