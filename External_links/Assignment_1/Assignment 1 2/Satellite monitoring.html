OVERVIEW


The most important international cause was held during the 1980s, to reduce the damage on earth's ozone layer by decreasing the emission of chlorofluorocarbons.


Ozone is formed in the stratosphere, concentration at 15-30 km above the surface of the Earth and helps in protecting us from the UV radiation from reaching its earth's surface and us on exposure on UV rays which causes Skin Cancer and other damages to living things on earth. Our atmosphere is fragile as given evidence by yearly ozone hole over Antarctica.


The CFCs so-called the Ozone depleting substances were used in refrigerants and aerosol cans and were emitted out causing the first Ozone depletion in the year 1989. further in the year 2011 there was a large amount of depletion of Ozone. Scientists used satellites to predict their future behaviour.


Satellites have been monitoring the Ozone layer using the European satellites like MetOp, ERS-2 and Envisat, which records the data and gets delivered to the scientists.


The Global Ozone Monitoring Experiment, or GOME-2, is an instrument carried by EUMETSAT's Metop-A which was launched in 2006 and Metop-B (2012) satellites, which are in a sun-synchronous polar orbit about 817 km above the Earth's surface and are specifically designed to measure ozone in the atmosphere.


Since 1979, satellite observations of the total ozone column, or the entire thickness of the ozone layer, have been the main source of daily observations on a worldwide scale for ozone layer monitoring.


The total ozone column, however, doesn't reveal anything regarding how ozone changes with height. The latter is crucial because it determines when and how ozone depletion occurs as well as the anticipated location of the first recovery signs. Additionally, altitude determines how climate influences stratospheric ozone.


The way that ozone varies with altitude can be observed using a variety of methods. The vertical distribution of ozone can also be determined by using downward-facing satellites and their instruments, especially those that measure reflected visible and ultraviolet solar radiation, like OMI, SCIAMACHY, GOME-2, and Tropomi from Copernicus Sentinel-5P.
