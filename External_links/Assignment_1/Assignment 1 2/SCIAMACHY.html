The SCIAMACHY


The Scanning Imaging Absorption Spectrometer for Atmospheric Cartography (SCIAMACHY) is an Imaging spectrometer which their primary object to was to perform global measurements to trace gases from stratosphere and troposphere. It calculates its Measurements in form of wavelengths range between 240 nm to 2400 nm. The goal of SCIAMACHY was to advance our understanding of a wide range of topics crucial to the chemistry and physics of the atmosphere (the troposphere, stratosphere, and mesosphere) and possible alterations brought about by either natural or man-made phenomena, including:


• Stratospheric Ozone Focus on Atlantic Ozone hole
• Troposphere Pollution by industrial activities
• Exchange between stratosphere and troposphere
• And events like solar proton events , and related global and regional phenomena

1. Instrument Objectives:


The measurements from SCIAMACHY made it possible to investigate a variety of phenomena that affect atmospheric chemistry:


• Troposphere: Biomass burning, pollution arctic haze, forest fires, dust storms, industrial plumes.


• Stratosphere: ozone chemistry, volcanic events, and solar proton events.


2. Secondary Instrument Objective:


• Measurements of aerosols:
SCIAMACHY's wide wavelength range made it the perfect tool for figuring out what kind of aerosols are in the atmosphere. The polarization data obtained from the PMDs (polarization measurement devices) helped with this analysis.


• Measurements of clouds:
O2, O4, and CO2 absorptions in nadir viewing provide information about light penetration in the atmosphere and, consequently, an estimate of the cloud top height. The cloud top height is also obtained by combining PMDs data. SCIAMACHY made it easy to measure Polar Stratospheric Clouds (PSCs), which are crucial to the ozone depletion process.


• Measurements of land and ocean:
SCIAMACHY gives the data on the surface spectral reflectance for large-scale processes between range of 0.33 µm to 2.5 µm.
