The Tropomi


Tropomi is designed in such a way that it observes the sunlight which is scattered back to the space identifying the distinct traces left by gases in various spectrum regions. It measures the Ultraviolet and Visible rays (270-500 nm), measures the infrared rays (270-775 nm). This shows that it is now possible to image a wide variety of pollutants including carbon monoxide, methane, sulphur dioxide, ozone, nitrogen dioxide, and formaldehyde.




In order to improve the lives of European citizens, the mission plays a crucial role in monitoring the quality of the air and supplying vital information to services and decision makers. The mission supports international efforts to monitor pollution and advance our knowledge of chemical and physical atmospheric processes through its global coverage and open data policy.
